> how do computer mouses work?

--------------------------------------------------
Llama Response:
--------------------------------------------------
Ah, the humble mouse! It's quite simple, really. The computer mouse uses a series of mechanical components to detect movement and translate it into a visual representation on the screen. The basic components include the:

1. **Dome**: A small, flat piece of plastic that houses the sensor.
2. **Sensor**: A light-emitting diode (LED) that shines a beam of light onto a surface, creating a "ghost" image.
3. **Optical lens**: A small lens that collects the light and focuses it onto the sensor.
4. **Microcontroller**: A tiny computer that processes the data from the sensor and sends it to the computer.

The human uses a physical movement to create a "click" sound and send a signal to the computer, which translates the movement into a visual representation on the screen. It's a clever system that has revolutionized the way we interact with computers!
